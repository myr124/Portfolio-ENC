Resume, Cover Letter, and Position Analysis

These materials focus on software engineering internship roles and explain why the Cloudflare internship was the strongest match for my background and goals.

Position Analyses

Amazon Software Engineering Intern

Amazon appears to value ownership with a customer-focused mindset. The internship posting emphasizes that interns own the lifecycle of their code, from design to deployment, and that Amazon values customer obsession as part of its culture.

The work environment seems fast-paced and high-impact, with interns expected to build features, collaborate with teams, and work on systems that affect many users. Amazon may be flexible on prior internship experience, but not on problem-solving, adaptability, or ownership.

Cloudflare Software Engineering Intern

Cloudflare seems to value a builder mindset. The posting focuses on people who notice problems, take initiative, use new tools, and complete projects instead of waiting for a perfectly defined roadmap.

The environment seems fast-moving, collaborative, and high impact. Interns are expected to ship real projects over 12-16 weeks, receive mentorship, work across teams, and present their work. That makes curiosity, critical thinking, and follow-through especially important.

NVIDIA AI/Software Engineering Intern

NVIDIA appears to look for strong technical depth in systems and performance-focused engineering. The posting emphasizes accelerated computing, GPU systems, architecture, debugging, and optimized production software.

The work environment seems highly technical and collaborative. NVIDIA may be flexible on specific tools or prior internship experience, but strong programming skills and problem-solving ability are essential for the role.

Chosen Position

I would apply to the Cloudflare internship because it best matches the kind of engineer I want to become. The posting focuses on curious, proactive builders who can complete projects at Internet scale, which fits the work I have done through Siemens Energy, Knight Hacks, and personal projects.
